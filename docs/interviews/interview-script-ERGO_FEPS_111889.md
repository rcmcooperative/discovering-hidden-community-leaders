# Interview script
## Introduction (2 min)

We’re exploring the scope, value, and impact of Research Community Management (RCM) work undertaken by Research Technical Professionals (RTPs) within the UK research ecosystem, particularly where this work is informal and unrecognised.

We'd like to ask you some questions to understand your experience. This is a semi structured interview so we may ask you some additional questions to clarify your answers or deepen our understanding in a specific direction.

The interview will last approximately 60 minutes. At the end of the interview, you will have a chance to tell us about any other aspect or experience which you think it would be important for us to know to understand this topic.

We will record the audio and use AI transcription and Microsoft Teams. I may also take notes in another document. Anything you say will remain confidential and you will not be identifiable in the analysis or publication relating to this interview.

We have previously confirmed that you are eligible, and you have told us about the communities you support and in which ways as part of the landscape mapping exercise. There you provided your consent to participate in this interview. Are you still happy to participate?

Do you have any questions before we begin?

## Question 1: Career backstory (5 min)

**What is your current professional role and history of engagement with communities?**

## Question 2: Activities undertaken (10 min)

*(Research Question: What types of RCM activities are RTPs undertaking, within or outside of your job description, and how do they map to the community lifecycle or maturity?)*

**a) In the landscape mapping, you said you have had responsibility for \[WP1 answers\] Tell me a bit about that work. Can you describe \[specific responsibility\] in more detail?**

**b) Have you done these as a new community is forming, when it is well established, or at any other point in a “community lifecycle”?**

## Question 3: Benefits of recognition (10 min)

*(Research Question: What are the perceived benefits and impacts of recognition of RCM activities on research communities and the wider research ecosystem?)*

**How is your undertaking of these activities recognised? Where they are recognised, how has that been beneficial to you, the community, or the wider ecosystem?**

## Question 4: Lack of recognition (10 min)

How does the lack of formal recognition and institutional support effect community sustainability, leadership opportunities and career progression, including progression and motivations into current roles?

**Where you haven’t had any recognition of these activities (or it has not been meaningful), how has that impacted the community or your own career progression?**

## Question 5: Competencies (10 min)

*(Research Question: What competencies and skills are required for effective RCM, particularly in relation to Community and Outreach competencies within the DIRECT Framework?)*

**We think skills and activities you identified in the mapping form are important to RCM work. Are there any you think are missing? How have you developed these skills? How would go about developing them further if you wanted to?**

## Question 6: Anything else (5 min)

Is there anything else we should have asked or you think we should understand?
