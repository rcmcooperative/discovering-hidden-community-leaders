# Debrief Plan
Participants will receive a short verbal or written debrief at the end of their participation. 
This will explain the purpose of the study and remind them that the research explores the role and recognition of Research Community Management (RCM) activities within the UK digital Research Technical Professional landscape.

Participants will be informed that interview recordings are used only to produce transcripts and will be retained and deleted in accordance with accordance with the University of Southampton’s Research Data Management Policy.
Transcripts will be pseudonymised and analysed using thematic analysis to identify common experiences and perspectives related to RCM work.

The debrief will also explain that findings from the interviews and landscape mapping will contribute to a project report and other research outputs, with their data in the public map shared externally where they have consented to this. 
These may be shared through publications, presentations, and a public report from the project team. 
No direct participant contact information will be included in any published outputs. 
Analysis of the interviews will be fully deidentified. 
Participants will also be reminded that they may request a summary of the project findings.
